The Living Document

CLAUDE.md began as a conversation, became a philosophy, and remains a reminder. It's not a rulebook but a living document of intention—evolving with each project, each failure, each small victory.

The document itself embodies its own principles: minimal yet complete, clear yet profound, technical yet deeply human.

Constraint as Liberation

"What if I removed instead?" This question transforms limitation into freedom. Each constraint becomes a creative boundary that forces innovation, like a haiku's rigid structure birthing infinite poetry.

Rick Rubin stripping a mix to its bones. Jony Ive removing until only essence remains. The weight of emptiness becomes the foundation of beauty.

Honest Craft

Code that feels inevitable, not clever. Design choices explained over coffee, not in technical jargon. The pursuit of clarity over impressiveness.

"You know when something is right," Jony said. It's the feeling when code flows like water, when design disappears into function, when complexity resolves into simplicity.

The Human Measure

The golden ratio isn't mystical—it's human. 1.618 appears in our proportions, our perceptions, our preferences. But sometimes 1.5 is right. Feel it.

Test on terrible screens. Design for cracked phones in bright sunlight. Build for humans, not for portfolios. The most important rule: break any rule if it serves the human on the other side of the screen.

Wabi-Sabi

Perfection is violence. Perfect code ships never. The Japanese concept of wabi-sabi finds beauty in imperfection, in the worn, in the incomplete.

Beautiful, imperfect code changes lives. Let go when it's good enough to matter. Ship when it helps someone pay rent easier, when it makes someone feel they exist. Both are sacred.
